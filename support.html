Frequently Asked Questions

Question 1: What is the difference between a savings account and a checking account?

A savings account is designed for accumulating and saving money, often earning interest.
A checking account, on the other hand, is more transactional, used for daily expenses with features like checks and debit cards.
Savings accounts typically have higher interest rates, while checking accounts offer more flexibility for withdrawals.

Question 2: How can I improve my credit score?

To improve your credit score, focus on paying bills on time, reducing outstanding debt, and maintaining a mix of different types of credit.
Regularly check your credit report for errors and address any discrepancies.

Question 3: How can I start saving money?

Begin by setting aside a small amount regularly. Consider opening a savings account, and make a budget to track your expenses and find areas to save.
